TACTICS
Tactics are methods whose validity is measured by their applicability to the situation at hand (Roberts 2018). In The Practice of Everyday Life (1984) Michel de Certeau describes everyday practices such as talking, reading, and moving about as tactical, “ways of operating” the Greeks called mētis (xix). The tactics of everyday practices are opportunistic; appropriating the hegemonic system from within, such “surreptitious creativities” form a “proliferating illegitimacy” which resists the authority that seeks their administration or suppression (de Certeau 1984, 96). A tactic poaches the system. James Scott (1998) elaborates mētis as practical knowledge. In contrast with epistemologies that are abstract, general, and universal, practical knowledge is experiential, situated, and local (Scott 1998, Chapter 9). Appropriate use of tactics depends on experiential knowledge gleaned through everyday navigations of terrains physical, conceptual, and digital. In other words, tactics deploy practical knowledge. For example, over the summer a friend of mine asked if I would print my artwork on tote bags. Looking into numerous custom printing websites, it seemed that with the complexity of my designs and color palette, each bag would cost me over fifty dollars. Unsure what to do, I then remembered seeing blank canvas tote bags for sale at a nearby crafts store for three dollars a piece. I also remembered that as a kid, I'd gotten so into dinosaurs that with the help of my baba I'd printed my drawings on transfer paper and ironed different dinosaurs onto T-shirts. I thought, why not print my own tote bags? I soon began experimenting with clothing, printing on T-shirts I gleaned from giveaway boxes by the curbside or what I found in fabric recycling dumpsters. I printed my designs using my Epson Workforce-Pro 4740 printer I gound in an alleyway shortly after moving to Vancouver. This page is an introduction to my tactics of practice and their significance for interfering with hegemonies of knowledge production from within.

TACTICS AS TRANSGRESSION



Generative Exercises (for when things feel stuck), a list to add to -walk the alley with a medium (voice recorder, sketchbook, camera, etc) -go outside and map something with paper and pen; map it again differently -choose one concept and free write for 20 minutes about it -mindmap with sticky notes on the wall -draw with chalk -write while sitting in a different location, with a change of view -perform a dérive for a day -exchange ideas with a friend over coffee -ride the bus without destination -hand-map something small scale and something large scale -from a plaza or rooftop parkade, look down to the street and observe what flows below -stand midway across a bridge and observe movement in all dimensions -remain at an intersection for 10 minutes and notice what patterns and disruptions emerge
